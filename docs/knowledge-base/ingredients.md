# Ingredients & sourcing — Sericia

## Where our food comes from

Every Sericia drop is a **single-producer run** from a Japanese maker we have personally contacted. We don't blend, we don't private-label, we don't re-pack. What you receive is what the maker packed — we add a hand-written origin card and ship it.

Current producer regions (drops rotate):

- **Kyoto** — sencha green tea (Wazuka), white miso (Fushimi)
- **Shizuoka** — sencha and gyokuro green tea
- **Nagano** — dried shiitake, enoki, maitake
- **Hokkaido** — white miso and kelp (dashi-grade)
- **Kyushu (Oita, Kumamoto)** — barley miso, yuzu kosho, fermented seasonings

## Allergens

All Sericia items are labeled on the drop page with the specific allergens present in **that drop's** batch. Japan has 7 statutory allergens (egg, milk, wheat, buckwheat, peanut, shrimp, crab) and 21 recommended declarations — we list both.

Common Sericia allergen profile by category:

| Category | Typical allergens |
|---|---|
| Sencha / matcha tea | None (no wheat, no soy) |
| Shiitake / mushroom | None |
| White miso | **Soy**, **wheat** (barley-miso = barley only) |
| Yuzu kosho / seasonings | Varies — see drop page |

If you have a severe allergy, **read the individual drop page's allergen panel** before buying. When in doubt, email <contact@sericia.com> with your concern and we'll check the maker's spec sheet directly.

## Shelf life

Every drop lists two dates:

- **Best-before (賞味期限)**: flavor is at its peak until this date.
- **Minimum remaining at dispatch**: we guarantee at least this much shelf life when the parcel leaves Japan.

Typical minimums by category:

| Category | Minimum shelf life on arrival |
|---|---|
| Sencha / matcha (unopened) | 90 days |
| Dried shiitake | 120 days |
| White miso (refrigerated) | 60 days |
| Yuzu kosho / seasonings | 90 days |

Once opened, consume per the maker's instruction on the package (typically: refrigerate and use within 2–4 weeks).

## Why drops are limited

We work with small makers who produce in seasonal batches. When the Wazuka producer finishes their spring first-flush sencha, that specific lot is done — the next lot is a new season, new harvest, often a different flavor profile. We'd rather sell the 200 tins that truly reflect one moment than dilute the story with a year-round blend.

This is also why we don't restock a sold-out drop. If you want to be notified when we source the same producer's next lot, use the "Notify me" form on the sold-out drop page.

## Certifications

Sericia is not organic-certified as a brand — individual drops carry their producer's certifications where applicable:

- **有機JAS (Organic JAS)**: Japan's national organic certification. Present on some sencha and miso drops.
- **JAS** (non-organic): quality standard for tea, miso.
- **Halal / Kosher**: none of our current producers are certified. Fermented miso uses koji (Aspergillus oryzae) but no alcohol is added post-fermentation — please verify your own authority's ruling.
