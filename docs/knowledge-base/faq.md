# FAQ — Sericia

## About the brand

### What is Sericia?

Sericia is a limited-edition drop platform for Japanese craft food. We source small-batch sencha, miso, dried shiitake, yuzu kosho and other producer-specific items, each in runs of 50–300 units, and ship them worldwide from Kyoto.

Every drop is single-producer and traceable to a prefecture, a maker, and a harvest or batch date.

### Why "drops" instead of a regular catalog?

Most Japanese craft makers produce seasonally. A true first-flush sencha is a 3-week window. A miso lot is a 6-month ferment, then it's sold. Rather than dilute the catalog with year-round house blends, Sericia publishes each producer's lot as a dated drop — when it's gone, it's gone, and the next drop is a new season or a new producer.

### Who's behind Sericia?

Sericia is run by [Paradigm LLC](https://paradigmjp.com), a Japan-based technology company. The food curation team is small (<5 people) and every drop is personally tasted before we list it.

## Ordering

### What payment methods do you accept?

### Can I change or cancel my order after paying?

If the parcel hasn't been dispatched, yes — email <contact@sericia.com> within 6 hours of ordering and we'll refund or modify. After dispatch (tracking number sent to your email), the parcel is with Japan Post and we can't recall it.

### Do you charge sales tax / VAT?

- **Japan orders**: 10% consumption tax is included in the list price.
- **International orders**: prices shown are VAT / sales-tax exclusive. Your country's customs may assess import VAT on delivery — see [shipping.md](./shipping.md) for de minimis thresholds.

### Can I ship to a different address than my billing address?

Yes. The checkout form has separate billing and shipping address fields.

## The food itself

### Are ingredients safe / tested for radiation?

Every producer we work with tests batches per Japanese food-safety law. Post-2011 radiation screening is standard for all food leaving Japan — levels are published on each drop page when the producer tests above the detection threshold (almost always they're below it).

### Is it all vegan / vegetarian?

Most Sericia drops are plant-based (tea, mushroom, miso, seasonings). We flag non-vegan drops explicitly — fish-based dashi or katsuobushi items are clearly labeled on the drop page. No drop is tested on animals.

### Is it organic?

Some drops are 有機JAS (Japan organic) certified — flagged on the drop page with the JAS symbol. Others are produced with minimal intervention but not formally certified (common for small makers where certification cost > benefit). See [ingredients.md](./ingredients.md) for detail.

### How should I store it at home?

- **Sencha / matcha**: airtight container, away from light, ideally refrigerated once opened.
- **Miso**: refrigerate after opening; lasts 3–6 months refrigerated.
- **Dried shiitake**: airtight container, cool dry place — keeps indefinitely.
- **Yuzu kosho / fermented seasonings**: refrigerate after opening.
